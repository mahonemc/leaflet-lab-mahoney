Virginia Census Data 2010-2017

Background

The Commonwealth of Virginia is a state in the Mid-Atlantic or the southeastern portion of the United States of America. It has undergone some substanital changes in terms of populationchanges in the last 20 years, especially since 2010. The northern portion of Virginia has expanded considerably and is now considered part of the Washington, D.C. metro area. The United States Census Bureau estimates that the state population was 8,411,808 on July 1, 2016, a 5.1% increase since the 2010 United States Census. This includes an increase from net migration of 381,969 people into the Commonwealth since the 2010 census.
Immigration from outside the United States resulted in a net increase of 159,627 people, and migration within the country produced a net increase of 155,205 people. Aside from Virginia, the top birth state for Virginians is New York, having overtaken North Carolina in the 1990s, with the Northeast accounting for the largest number of migrants into the state by region.[126]

Recent Changes

According to a study by the University of Virginia, most population increases occured in the Northern Virginia, Richmond and Hampton Roads metropolitan areas. Northern Virginia accounts for more than 60 percent of the population change since 2011. Another big increase in population happened in the Charlottesville Metropolitan Area, where population increased by 6.3 percent since 2010.
